## 1. TRIAL PHASE OVERVIEW

### Purpose
The trial phase serves four critical functions:
1. **Validate the platform** with real agents making real bookings
2. **Gather feedback** to refine tools, training, and processes before full launch
3. **Build case studies** — "Agent X booked $Y in their first month with Trip Tailors"
4. **Filter out time wasters** — only serious agents should progress to paid membership

### Trial Structure
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Duration | 3 months (Months 4-6 post-funding) |
| Target agents | 10-20 |
| Signup fee | $0 (waived) |
| Monthly fee | $0 (waived) |
| Commission split | 85/15 (agent keeps 85%, TT keeps 15%) |
| Lead marketplace access | Operates as standard |
| Minimum activity requirement | 1 booking attempt within 30 days |
| Training completion | Mandatory (all modules) |
| Platform feedback | Required (monthly survey) |

### Why Free (Not Discounted)?
- $299 signup fee is a barrier when the platform is unproven
- Free removes all risk for agents, maximizing sign-up rate
- The 85/15 commission split still generates revenue for Trip Tailors (better than the standard 70/30-80/20 of competitors)
- Feedback from free agents is more honest — they have no buyer's remorse bias
- Case study value far exceeds the $839 in waived fees

### Why Not Completely Free Forever?
- A commission split still applies (15%) — agents have skin in the game
- Mandatory training and activity requirements filter casual sign-ups
- Clear end date (3 months) creates urgency to engage
- Conversion to paid subscription validates willingness to pay

## 2. AGENT SELECTION CRITERIA

### Who to Target for Trial
Not all agents are equal. Target agents who will:
1. Actually use the platform (not just sign up and disappear)
2. Provide constructive feedback
3. Have existing clients or leads (can start booking immediately)
4. Represent diverse markets (UK, EU, US, GCC)

### Ideal Trial Agent Profile
| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|-----------|-------|
| Experience | 1-5 years | <1 year (motivated) | No interest in travel |
| Existing credentials | IATA/ATOL/equivalent | In process of getting | None and no plans |
| Indemnity insurance | Active policy | Willing to get | Unwilling |
| Current booking volume | 2-5+/month | 1-2/month | 0 (no history) |
| Tech comfort | Uses CRM/digital tools | Willing to learn | Resistant to tech |
| Geography | UK, US, EU, GCC | Any English-speaking | Non-English (initially) |
| Motivation | Build their business | Try something new | "Just looking" |

### Selection Process
1. **Application Form** (online, 10 minutes):
   - Name, location, contact details
   - Current credentials (IATA number, ATOL, etc.)
   - Insurance details
   - Willingness to self insure
   - Current monthly booking volume
   - Annual Profits
   - Why they want to join Trip Tailors
   - Social media presence (optional but valued)

2. **Screening Call** (15-20 minutes):
   - Verify credentials and insurance
   - Assess motivation and fit
   - Explain platform, tools, expectations
   - Confirm commitment to 3-month trial

3. **Acceptance Email** with:
   - Trial agreement (simplified contract)
   - Onboarding schedule
   - Login credentials
   - Training module links

**Target: 25-30 applications → 15-20 accepted → 12-15 active by end of trial**

## 4. TIME WASTER FILTERING

### Activity Requirements (Trial Period)
To remain in the trial, agents must:

| Requirement | Deadline | Consequence of Non-Compliance |
|------------|----------|-------------------------------|
| Complete all 6 training modules | End of Week 3 | Warning → removal if not completed by Week 4 |
| Create first sample itinerary | End of Week 2 | Gentle reminder |
| Access platform at least 3x/week | Ongoing | Flag for follow-up |
| Attempt at least 1 booking | End of Month 1 | Check-in call; if no intent, graceful exit |
| Submit monthly feedback survey | End of each month | Reminder → restrict lead access |
| Respond to communications within 48 hours | Ongoing | Flag for follow-up |

### Red Flags (Remove from Trial)
- No login for 2+ weeks without explanation
- No booking attempt after 6 weeks
- Failure to complete training after reminders
- Unprofessional conduct or misrepresentation
- Fraudulent credential claims

### Graceful Exit Process
1. Personal email from founder explaining the decision
2. Thank them for their time
3. Offer to reconsider if circumstances change
4. Deactivate account access
5. No negative consequences or public mention

## 5. TRIAL-TO-PAID CONVERSION

### Conversion Timeline
| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| Week 8 | Email all trial agents: "Your trial ends in 4 weeks. Here's what you've achieved..." |
| Week 9 | Personal check-in call with each agent: feedback, satisfaction, conversion discussion |

| Week 11 | Follow-up with undecided agents; address concerns |
| Week 12 | Trial ends; converting agents move to paid; non-converting agents offboarded |
| Week 13 | Grace period (2 weeks) for late deciders |

### Conversion Offer (Standard)
| Item | Trial | Paid |
|------|-------|------|
| Signup fee | $0 | **$149** (50% off standard $299 for trial converts) |
| Monthly fee | $0 | $45/month |
| Commission split | 85/15 | 75/25 (standard - if booking volume corresponds to another tier upgrade them early as a gesture of goodwill) |
| Lead marketplace | 5 free/month | Pay per lead ($5-$20) |

### Conversion Incentives
1. **Early bird discount:** First 3 months at $35/month (not $45) for agents who convert before trial ends
2. **Loyalty credit:** $100 credit toward lead marketplace purchases
3. **Grandfathered commission:** Keep 85/15 for 3 more months, then standard 75/25
4. **Testimonial bonus:** $50 credit for agents who provide a written testimonial

### Target Conversion Rate: 75%
- 15 trial agents × 75% = 11-12 paid agents
- These 12 agents become the foundation; their testimonials and case studies fuel recruitment

## 6. COMMISSION TIER STRUCTURE — DETAILED

### Tier Definitions

| Tier | Annual Comission and planning fee agent earnings Value | Commission Split (Agent/TT) |
|------|---------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Starter** | $0-$40,000 | 75 / 25 |
| **Rising** | $40,000-$60,000 | 80 / 20 |
| **Expert** | $60,000-$100,000 | 85 / 15 |
| **Elite** | $100,000 | 90 / 10 |

### What Counts as a "Qualifying Booking"
A booking qualifies for tier progression when ALL of the following are met:
1. Client has paid the planning fee and/or comission on bookings and Trip Tailors recieves their cut.
2. Booking was made through Trip Tailors' platform (RateHawk integration or approved external platform)
3. Booking was not cancelled or fully refunded and is completed (the client completes the vacation).
4. Minimum booking comission and planning fee earned: $200 (client price)

**Partial refunds:** If a booking is partially refunded but the remaining value is >$200, it still counts.


### Tier Display in Agent Dashboard
Each agent should see:
- Current tier (badge + name)
- Current qualifying bookings count & value
- Bookings needed for next tier
- Progress bar (visual)
- Current commission split
- Next tier split (motivation)

Example: "You're a **Rising Agent** with **47 qualifying bookings**. **28 more bookings** to reach Expert (90/10 split)! [===========-------] 63%"

## 7. LEAD MARKETPLACE — PRICING & RULES

### Lead Pricing Tiers

### Lead Marketplace
Leads generated from the Referral Hub and Public Website are sold to agents:
| Lead Strength | Price | Description |
|---------------|-------|-------------|
| Standard | $5 | Basic inquiry, minimal detail, low budget |
| Qualified | $10 | Modest budget, details filled out correctly |
| Premium | $20 | High budget, specific requirements, detailed request |

### Lead Marketplace Rules
1. **First-come, first-served:** Leads are visible to all agents; first to purchase gets exclusive access
2. **No refunds on leads:** Once purchased, the lead is yours (prevents gaming)
3. **Lead freshness:** Leads expire after 3 days if not purchased; given to a selected agent for free
4. **Exclusive access:** Each lead is sold to ONE agent only (builds trust in the marketplace)
5. **Feedback required:** Agent must update lead status (contacted, quoted, booked, lost), if not tracked after 30 days automated email sent asking the reason and prompting them to update.
6. **Quality guarantee:** If a lead's contact information is invalid (wrong email/phone), Trip Tailors issues a credit, agent must provide supporting evidence.

### Lead Sources
| Source | Expected Volume | Quality |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| Referral network (TikTok, social) | 60% of leads | Medium-High (warm, interested) |
| Public website (organic) | 25% of leads | Medium (actively searching) |
| SEO/content marketing | 10% of leads | Medium-High (intent-based) |
| Paid advertising | 5% of leads | Variable |

### Lead Economics
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Cost to acquire lead (referral) | $50 per booked + $3-5 marketing per lead generated |
| Cost to acquire lead (organic) | $0-$2 per lead |
| Average lead sell price | $10 |
| Leads sold per booking | ~5-7 (15-20% conversion) |
| Net margin on referral leads | Break-even to slightly positive |
| Net margin on organic leads | $8-$10 per lead (high margin) |

**The lead marketplace is a flywheel:** More agents → more demand for leads → more marketing investment justified → more leads → more agents attracted by lead availability
